N-Size Design Sketch

PSIR220 · In-Class Activity · Week 8 Session 2

What We're Doing Today

You've seen how N-size shapes method and what kinds of claims you can make. Today you'll:

  • Match research questions to appropriate N-sizes and methods (warm-up)
  • Sketch your own research design using the N-Size framework
  • Stress-test a partner's design as a "devil's advocate"

1Matching Warm-Up

For each research question below, choose the most appropriate N-size range and primary method. There may be more than one defensible answer — be ready to justify yours.

Does UN peacekeeping reduce civilian casualties? (150 missions, 1990–2020)
How did Mandela's rhetoric reshape South African national identity during the transition?
Why did Tunisia democratise after the Arab Spring while Egypt did not?
Which combination of conditions produces successful power-sharing in post-conflict states? (28 cases)

2Sketch Your Design

Now apply this to your own research project. Fill in every field — even if you're uncertain. Uncertainty is fine; leaving it blank is not.

If you haven't already, open the N-Size Slider tool on the course site and use the Paradigm → Method tab to check which methods align with your track.

Be specific: name the cases or describe the dataset
